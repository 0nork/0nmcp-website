What to Report

Types of Infringement

The following activities violate our intellectual property rights. If you see any of these, please report it immediately.

👤

Brand Impersonation

Creating accounts, profiles, or websites that claim to be 0nMCP, 0nORK, or any 0n-affiliated entity.

Example: A Twitter/X account named "@0nmcp_official" not operated by RocketOpp
📦

Package Squatting

Publishing packages on npm, PyPI, or other registries using names that are identical or confusingly similar to 0n marks.

Example: An npm package named "0nmcp-pro" or "onmcp" not published by 0nork
🌐

Domain Cybersquatting

Registering domain names containing "0nmcp", "0nork", or variations to profit from brand confusion.

Example: Domains like "0nmcp.io", "0nork.app", "0nmcp-download.com"
🛠

Code Rebranding

Forking the open-source code and redistributing it under a confusingly similar name or with 0n branding intact.

Example: A GitHub repo called "0nMCP-Enhanced" or "0nMCP-Fork" used commercially
⚖

Patent Infringement

Implementing the patented Three-Level Execution Hierarchy or MCP Federation architecture without license.

Example: A competing product using Pipeline → Assembly Line → Radial Burst terminology
💰

Unauthorized Commercial Use

Using 0n marks, technology, or branding to promote, sell, or market products or services without written consent.

Example: An agency advertising "Powered by 0nMCP" without a partnership agreement

What Happens Next

Our Enforcement Process

Every report is taken seriously. Here is our standard enforcement timeline after receiving a valid report.

1

Report Received & Triaged

Your report is logged in our legal tracking system and assigned to an investigator. You receive a confirmation email.

Within 24 hours
2

Investigation & Documentation

We capture evidence including screenshots, WHOIS records, archived pages (Wayback Machine), and technical analysis. All evidence is preserved for potential litigation.

24 – 48 hours
3

Cease-and-Desist Issued

A formal cease-and-desist letter is sent to the infringer (and their hosting provider, if applicable) demanding immediate removal of infringing content.

48 – 72 hours
4

Platform Takedown Requests

DMCA notices filed with GitHub, npm, hosting providers, and domain registrars. Abuse reports submitted to social media platforms.

72 hours – 1 week
5

Legal Escalation

If the infringement is not resolved through cease-and-desist and takedown notices, the matter is referred to legal counsel for federal court filing, UDRP proceedings, or other appropriate judicial action.

1 – 2 weeks
